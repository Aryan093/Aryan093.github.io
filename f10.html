Euglenoids are single-celled organisms with a flexible body, often equipped with a flagellum for movement.

Euglenoids are a diverse group of single-celled microorganisms belonging to the phylum Euglenophyta. These microscopic organisms are known for their unique characteristics, including a flexible body that can change shape, often propelled by a whip-like tail called a flagellum. Euglenoids can be found in a variety of aquatic environments, including freshwater bodies and even some moist soils. While some euglenoids are photosynthetic and contain chloroplasts, allowing them to produce their own food through photosynthesis, others are heterotrophic and feed on smaller microorganisms. These adaptive features make euglenoids fascinating subjects for scientific study. They are important contributors to the diversity of life in aquatic ecosystems and play roles in nutrient cycling and energy flow within these environments.
