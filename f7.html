Dinophysiales is an order of dinoflagellates, which are single-celled aquatic microorganisms known for their characteristic two flagella and diverse ecological roles. Some Dinophysiales species are known for their involvement in harmful algal blooms (HABs), producing potent toxins that can harm marine life and pose risks to human health through contaminated seafood consumption. These harmful species can disrupt aquatic ecosystems and impact fisheries. However, not all Dinophysiales are harmful; some play essential roles in marine food webs, serving as a source of nutrition for various aquatic organisms. Scientists closely monitor these dinoflagellates due to their ecological significance and the potential environmental and health risks associated with HABs.
