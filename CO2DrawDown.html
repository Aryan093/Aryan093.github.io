Climate Regulation

Climate regulation refers to the mechanisms and processes that help maintain the Earth's temperature and climate within a relatively stable range, allowing for habitable conditions on our planet. These mechanisms involve various natural processes, as well as the influence of human activities.

Oxygen Production

Oxygen production refers to the natural process by which oxygen (O2) gas is generated in various environments, primarily through photosynthesis. Oxygen is essential for sustaining life on Earth, as it serves as a critical component of the atmosphere and is required for the respiration of most living organisms.

Marine Food Web

A marine food web is a complex, interconnected system that illustrates the transfer of energy and nutrients among various marine organisms within an aquatic ecosystem. It showcases how different species in the ocean interact with one another by either consuming or being consumed by other organisms.

Carbon Sequestration

Carbon sequestration is a natural or artificial process that captures and stores carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere, preventing it from contributing to global warming and climate change. This process plays a crucial role in mitigating the effects of greenhouse gas emissions.
