Coccolithophores are a type of microscopic phytoplankton distinguished by their calcium carbonate plates, known as coccoliths, which surround their cells. These tiny marine organisms play a significant role in the Earth's carbon cycle by sequestering carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere and, upon death, transporting it to the ocean floor. Their calcareous shells contribute to the formation of chalk and limestone deposits on the ocean floor over geological time scales. Coccolithophores are an essential part of marine ecosystems and play a role in regulating the planet's climate through their influence on the carbon balance in the oceans. Additionally, they are a valuable area of research for understanding the impacts of ocean acidification, a consequence of increased CO2 levels in the atmosphere.
